## Summary

The party met at the Coppergate Market in the city of Verenmoor, each having been separately drawn there by rumor, obligation, or accident. A wandering tinker sold them a small ornate key — said to open "any door worth opening" — for a price that seemed too reasonable. That evening, the key began to hum.

## Events

- **The Coppergate Market** — A sprawling outdoor market in Verenmoor's merchant quarter. The party converged here by separate paths.
- **Aldus the Tinker** — A cheerful, evasive old man selling curiosities. The key was displayed in a locked glass case he opened with a flick of his wrist. He sold it to the highest bidder from the group (or whoever made the most interesting case) for twelve gold pieces.
- **The Hum** — At sundown, while the party was sharing a meal at the *Rusted Flagon*, the key grew warm and began vibrating. It pointed — like a compass — toward the city's old quarter.
- **The Locked Building** — Following the key's pull led them to a building that had no door. Just a blank stone wall with a keyhole.

## Questions Raised

- Who is Aldus the Tinker, and where did he get the key?
- What does the doorless building contain?
- Why does the key resonate now, after presumably sitting in a glass case?
