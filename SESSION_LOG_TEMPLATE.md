# Session Log Template – Player Facing (Standardized Format)

This document defines the required structure for all player-facing D&D session logs.

The format is fixed and should always use the same section headers in the same order.  
Consistency is required for campaign continuity, searchability, and archival clarity.

---

# Session Title

A short, evocative title reflecting the session’s main theme or event.

---

## TL;DR

A concise summary of the session in 2–4 sentences.

- Highlight the major conflict or achievement.
- Mention key NPCs or revelations.
- Keep it punchy and readable.

---

## Session Summary

A cinematic but clear narrative overview of the session.

Guidelines:
- Write in past tense.
- Focus on major events.
- Avoid excessive mechanical detail.
- Preserve emotional tone and character moments.
- Maintain readability for players.

This section should feel like a story recap, not combat notes.

---

## Location / Phase Sections

Break the session into meaningful narrative segments.

Each section should:

- Use a clear heading reflecting the location or phase.
- Describe what happened.
- Highlight character actions.
- Include important outcomes.
- Mention key mechanical moments only when narratively significant.

Examples:
- Return to the Study
- The Revelry Chamber
- Pyre’s Forge
- The Gullet Escape

---

## Explorations and Discoveries

Document all major discoveries made during the session.

Include:
- Lore revelations
- Important NPC information
- Magical item discoveries
- Clues, secrets, and foreshadowing
- Changes to world state

This section preserves campaign continuity.

---

## What Comes Next

Document:
- Immediate next objective
- Unresolved tensions
- Open plot threads
- Questions left unanswered

This section maintains narrative momentum.

---

This ensures:
- Timeline consistency
- Cross-arc continuity
- Long-term narrative tracking

Format example:
`In-World Date: 14th of Eleasis, 1492 DR`

---

# Writing Rules

- Use Markdown formatting.
- Maintain consistent header hierarchy.
- Write clearly and concisely.
- Avoid em dashes.
- Keep tone immersive but structured.
- Do not deviate from section order.
- Do not omit sections.

This template is mandatory for all future player-facing session logs.
